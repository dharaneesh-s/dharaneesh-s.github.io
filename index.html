About me

Data & AI professional building pipelines, models, and dashboards that drive real decisions.

I'm Dharaneesh, originally from a rural area near Erode, Tamil Nadu, India. My upbringing was shaped by my grandparents in the village and my godparents in Phoenix, Arizona — a blend of rural simplicity and exposure to technology that still defines how I approach problems: grounded, practical, and always building.


With 5+ years of experience, I work end-to-end across the data stack: writing ETL pipelines, training ML models, and putting AI-powered tools in the hands of stakeholders. Currently at Harris County Government in Houston, I lead data engineering, data governance, and AI initiatives for the Housing & Community Development department — managing data across $400M+ in federal housing programs covering 5,000+ units. Before that, I built regulatory data warehouses at Infosys/Rabobank (150M+ records) and predictive models for D1 athletics at the University of Utah.


I hold a Master's in Business Analytics from the University of Utah's David Eccles School of Business, and I'm currently pursuing a part-time Doctorate in Business Administration focused on AI. I build things that work, ship them, and make them better.
